Interkom vs Slack: an honest comparison

Slack is the default. That doesn't make it the right fit for every team. Here's a straight comparison of Interkom vs Slack — including the cases where Slack is genuinely the better call.

We build Interkom, so treat this as a point of view rather than a neutral referee. We've tried to keep it fair: a comparison only helps if it tells you when not to switch. If you're weighing a Slack alternative more broadly, start with our guide — this piece is the head-to-head.

The short version

Interkom is a private, end-to-end encrypted team space built around calm, workspace-scoped communication with native apps and flat pricing. Slack is a mature, extensible platform with a huge app ecosystem and broad cross-company "Connect" features. Most small, privacy-conscious teams are happier on Interkom; large orgs deep in the Slack app ecosystem often aren't ready to move.

Where Interkom wins

  • Privacy. End-to-end encryption and a workspace with no public feed — easy to explain to your team and your lawyer.
  • Calm. Presence, reactions, voice notes and team stories reduce channel sprawl instead of adding to it.
  • Native feel. A real macOS app and a real iOS app — lighter on battery and memory than an Electron wrapper.
  • Predictable cost. Free for small teams, then one flat plan. No tiering puzzle.

Where Slack is still the better choice

Being honest about this is the whole point:

  • You live in the Slack app ecosystem. Hundreds of deep integrations and custom workflows are hard to replace.
  • Heavy cross-company collaboration. If Slack Connect with external orgs is core to how you work, that's a Slack strength today.
  • Large, established org. Migrating thousands of people and years of automation is a project, not a swap.
Rule of thumb: if your team is small, privacy matters, and you mostly need chat done well — Interkom. If you're a large org wired into the Slack marketplace — stay, for now.

Bottom line

For privacy-conscious small and mid-size teams, Interkom delivers the essentials of Slack with stronger privacy, a calmer default, native apps, and simpler pricing. For large orgs built on Slack's ecosystem, the switch is harder to justify yet. Read the broader guide to choosing a Slack alternative, or just try it.
